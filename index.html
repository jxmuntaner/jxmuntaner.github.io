Hi, I'm Joseph Xavier! I'm a Postdoctoral Scholar in Law and Economics. I'm simultaneously enrolled at the Universitat de Barcelona, where I'm finishing a master's to access the legal profession.

My research main research interests are in antitrust economics, with a special focus on killer acquisitions, network industries and two-sided markets, as well as health economics, financial regulation, contract theory, and white-collar crime. In essence, I mostly deal with the application of microeconomic and econometric methods to legal problems and policymaking to learn about how companies can alter the efficient functioning of markets and the economic consequences of enforcement.

Prior to moving back to Barcelona, I completed my PhD at the University of Yale. I lived in France, the United States and the United Kingdom, where I did my undergraduate studies ( Sciences Po - Institut d'études politiques de Paris, University of California, Berkeley) and my master's degree ( London Business School). I've been a recipient of the Fundación Ramon Areces Scholarship and several merit-based awards by the French Ministry of Education and Research, the London Business School and ESADE.

I have worked as an international tax consultant and as an economist at multinational pharmaceutical companies. I've also provided expert advice for several governments and the private sector.
